VAX Business Plan

A comprehensive plan for building the global verified marketplace for AI agents with proven ROI.

Executive Summary

Verified Agent Exchange (VAX)

Mission: Build the world's first trusted marketplace where every AI agent is verified for revenue generation, ROI, and reproducibility.

Problem: The AI agent market is growing at 45%+ CAGR, but buyers have no way to verify agent quality, leading to distrust and underinvestment. Builders have no distribution channel for their proven agents.

Solution: A global trading platform with mandatory revenue verification, a standardized 6-dimension trust score, and end-to-end deployment confidence.

Target: $216K revenue in 12 months (standard scenario) through 12% take rate, $500 verification fees, featured listings, and managed operations.

GTM: English-speaking AI agent builders using n8n, LangGraph, AutoGen, and Claude Code. Community-first launch with early access program.

Market Analysis
AI Agent Market Dynamics

Total Addressable Market

The global AI agent market is projected to reach $47B by 2028, growing at 45% CAGR. Enterprise adoption of autonomous agents is accelerating as companies seek operational efficiency.

Serviceable Market

English-speaking agent builders and buyers represent roughly 60% of the market. Our initial focus on Sales, Content, and Data agents targets the highest-ROI categories.

Buyer Demand

78% of enterprises plan to deploy AI agents by 2027. Key pain points: quality assurance (91%), ROI uncertainty (87%), and integration complexity (72%).

Supply Gap

Thousands of capable agent builders exist on platforms like n8n, LangGraph, and AutoGen, but lack distribution channels. Most sell through word-of-mouth or freelance platforms.

Risk Analysis
Key Risks & Mitigations
High

Chicken-and-Egg (Supply/Demand)

Marketplace needs both agents and buyers. Without agents, no buyers; without buyers, no agents.

Mitigation: Seed supply first with waived verification fees. Build 20+ agents in-house. Community partnerships with builder platforms.
High

Verification Accuracy

If our verification is wrong or gameable, the entire trust proposition collapses.

Mitigation: Multi-layer verification (documentation + live testing + ongoing monitoring). Start manual, automate gradually. Reputation staking.
Medium

Platform Competition

OpenAI, Google, or major platforms could build their own agent marketplace.

Mitigation: They won't verify revenue (conflict of interest). Our value is trust, not distribution. Network effects compound over time.
Medium

Disintermediation

Buyers and sellers could connect on VAX and then transact off-platform.

Mitigation: Ongoing monitoring and trust score updates require platform participation. Managed ops creates lock-in. Escrow protects both parties.
Low

Technical Execution

Building the marketplace itself is straightforward with chosen tech stack.

Mitigation: Proven stack (Next.js, Supabase, Stripe). MVP-first approach. 4-week sprint cycles.
Low

Regulatory

AI regulation could impact agent deployment or marketplace operations.

Mitigation: Verification process naturally aligns with regulatory trends toward AI transparency. Position as compliance-friendly.
